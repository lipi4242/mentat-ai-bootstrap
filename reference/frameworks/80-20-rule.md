# 80/20 Rule (Pareto Principle)

~80% of effects come from ~20% of causes. Heuristic, not a precise law. Use it to find the high-leverage wedge and cut the rest.

## What it is

Vilfredo Pareto observed that 80% of Italy's land was owned by 20% of the population. The pattern recurs across domains: 20% of customers generate 80% of revenue, 20% of bugs cause 80% of crashes, 20% of features drive 80% of usage. The numbers are illustrative — the point is steep imbalance. Most inputs are low-value noise; a small minority drive nearly all the outcome.

## When to apply

- Prioritizing a backlog or feature list
- Scoping a project (what to cut)
- Finding the wedge (where to start)
- Deciding what to stop doing
- Debugging (find the few causes of many failures)

Do **not** use it when coverage is the goal — e.g., compliance, safety checklists, or audit completeness.

## How to apply

1. **List candidate inputs.** Features, tasks, customers, bugs — whatever you're prioritizing.
2. **Estimate impact for each.** Rough is fine — relative rank matters more than precision. Use: revenue impact, user frequency, error rate, time saved, or any outcome metric.
3. **Rank by impact, descending.**
4. **Find the natural break.** Look for a step-change in impact between adjacent items. Everything below the break is a candidate for cutting, deferring, or ignoring.
5. **Cut ruthlessly.** The 80/20 insight is not just to identify the top items — it's to act on the implication that the bottom 80% of items are worth very little relative to their cost.

## One worked example

Feature backlog — 12 items ranked by estimated weekly active users impacted:

| # | Feature | Est. WAU impact |
|---|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Search | 4,200 |
| 2 | Notifications | 3,800 |
| 3 | Export to CSV | 2,100 |
| 4 | Dark mode | 410 |
| 5 | Custom colors | 290 |
| ... | (7 more) | < 200 each |

**Cut line** between items 3 and 4 — step-change from 2,100 to 410.

**Decision:** Scope sprint to items 1–3. They cover ~90% of WAU impact. Defer items 4–12 for a future cycle or drop them entirely.
